Physicians

Dr. Monica Lewinski

Family Physician. Family medicine is one of the primary care specialties. Family practice physicians see patients of all ages, provide basic care for a variety of common ailments, are usually the first to recognize major health problems, and may order diagnostic tests or refer to a specialist.

“A great physician must combine empathy with good listening and communication skills.”

Dr. Whitney Houston

Internal Medicine Physician. General internists provide primary care to adult patients and can take additional training after internal medicine residency to sub-specialize in a variety of other areas, such as gastroenterology, endocrinology, or cardiology.

“It is with joy and honor that I am able to associate with my patients in helping them achieve their best health possible.”

Dr. Pepper Potts

Pediatrician. Pediatricians take care of younger patients, from infancy through age 18 or, in some cases, age 21. Pediatricians provide primary health care to children, including immunizations, well-baby checks, school physicals, and treatment of coughs, colds, and stomach flu, among many other things. More seriously ill or complicated patients may be referred to a pediatric sub-specialist for specialized treatment.

“Love my job.”
